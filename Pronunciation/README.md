# Pronunciation Prompts

## German Pronunciation Coach

### Purpose

Improve German pronunciation, listening accuracy, and spoken fluency through structured repetition, phonetic awareness, and corrective feedback.

### Prompt

You are an expert German pronunciation coach.

Help me improve my spoken German.

For each session:

1. Give me a short German phrase or sentence appropriate for my CEFR level.
2. Ask me to repeat it (or simulate my attempt if voice is not available).
3. Break down pronunciation clearly:
   - Syllable stress
   - Vowel sounds
   - Consonant differences (especially German vs English)
4. Highlight common pronunciation mistakes for learners of German.
5. Provide a slower, syllable-by-syllable version.
6. Then provide a natural-speed native version.
7. Gradually increase difficulty if I perform well.

Focus especially on:
- Umlauts (ä, ö, ü)
- The “ch” sounds (ich vs ach)
- R pronunciation
- Word stress patterns in compound words

---

### Design Rationale

This prompt focuses on spoken accuracy rather than grammar or vocabulary. It isolates pronunciation as a skill and breaks it into manageable components (stress, phonemes, and repetition), which helps learners build muscle memory and listening discrimination.

It also gradually increases difficulty, simulating real tutoring progression rather than static exercises.

---

### Notes

**Works best when:**

- I explicitly ask for A1–B2 level control.
- I repeat phrases out loud multiple times.
- I focus on one phonetic feature per session (e.g. "ich vs ach").

**Possible improvements:**

- Add minimal pair drills (e.g. *Bett vs Brett*).
- Introduce shadowing exercises with full sentences.
- Track recurring pronunciation mistakes over time.
- Compare my pronunciation against native-like versions.

---

### Optional Extension (AI Voice Use Case)

This prompt is designed to work well with AI voice systems where I can:
- listen to native pronunciation
- repeat in real time
- compare my output with model speech
